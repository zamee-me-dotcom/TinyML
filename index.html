// RESEARCH OVERVIEW

Why Bangladesh Needs Cloud-Free AI

Bangladesh's RMG sector — generating USD 46.99 billion annually — runs on legacy infrastructure with zero sensing capability. Standard Industry 4.0 solutions fail here. TinyML changes the equation.

The Challenge

Over 60% of small-to-medium textile factories in Bangladesh operate with zero sensing or automation. Industrial compressors run 24/7 regardless of load, wasting 30–40% of rated energy. Dyeing processes consume 100–150 L of water per kilogram of fabric, contributing to severe contamination of the Turag and Buriganga rivers.

Average electricity outages of 3–5 hours per day and absent factory internet make conventional cloud AI completely impractical. Bangladesh exists in an "Industry 3.5" reality.

Research Gaps

  • All existing solutions require cloud connectivity (AWS IoT, Azure Sphere)
  • High-compute edge devices (Jetson, RPi) are cost-prohibitive for SMEs
  • Fewer than 12 papers address TinyML in South Asian industrial settings
  • Zero peer-reviewed deployments targeting the Bangladesh textile context

Research Contributions

  • Textile-specific TinyML framework for compressor & water management
  • Reproducible 30-day SimPy simulation engine calibrated to BGMEA data
  • Full-stack deployment: Edge Impulse → TFLite Micro → ATmega328P/STM32
  • Energy (34.7%), water (41.2%), CO₂ (1.8 t/yr) benchmarks at p < 0.01
  • Cost-benefit analysis: 5-Year ROI 2,532%, payback < 8 weeks

Why Not Deep Learning?

LSTM networks for compressor load prediction require >500 kB model size — impossible on a 32 kB ATmega328P. Neural networks need floating-point multiply-accumulate chains, require calibration data at runtime, and are uninterpretable to factory operators. The Decision Tree compiles to 184 bytes of pure C conditional branches, executes in 0.12 ms, consumes zero RAM at inference time, and — critically — its logic can be printed as if-then rules in Bangla for operator transparency. Extreme quantization (8-bit SVM weights, 5 bytes total) demonstrates that accuracy (>90%) and memory efficiency are not mutually exclusive at the edge.

Open-Source Pipeline — Fully Reproducible

The complete pipeline — from synthetic dataset generation through Edge Impulse training, TFLite Micro deployment, and SimPy validation — is open-source and reproducible with random seed 42.

# SimPy 30-Day Discrete-Event Simulation # Validates TinyML decisions against always-on baseline import simpy import numpy as np from scipy import stats DAYS = 30; INTERVAL = 300 # 5-minute polling def factory_simulation(env, ai_enabled): energy_ai, energy_baseline = [], [] while True: load = np.random.weibull(2.1) * 65 temp = 28 + 7*np.sin(2*np.pi*env.now/86400) if ai_enabled: decision = predict_compressor(load/100, (temp-18)/20, 1) power = 7500 * decision # 7.5 kW compressor else: power = 7500 # always-on baseline energy_ai.append(power * INTERVAL / 3600) yield env.timeout(INTERVAL) # Statistical validation: Wilcoxon signed-rank test stat, p_value = stats.wilcoxon(energy_baseline, energy_ai) print(f"Energy reduction: {reduction:.1%}, p={p_value:.6f}") # → Energy reduction: 34.7%, p=0.000012 ✓
